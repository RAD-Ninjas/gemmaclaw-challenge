# The Showcase

Ad-hoc community showcase at **Sports Page, Mountain View**.

---

## Format

This is casual and fun — not a formal presentation. Show up with your project, grab a drink, and share what you built.

- **Lightning talks**: 3 minutes max, hard cutoff
- **No slides required** — just demo your project live
- **Q&A**: Quick questions from the audience after each demo
- **Voting**: Audience votes on awards at the end

---

## RSVP

RSVP on the **[GDG Mountain View event page](#)** if you want to present. No pre-registration of your project is required — just show up.

---

## What to Bring

- Your hardware (Pi, laptop, phone — whatever you built on)
- A way to demo (screen, monitor, or just your laptop)
- Your code (ideally pushed to a GitHub repo or fork of this one)
- A 1-sentence elevator pitch for your project

---

## Lightning Talk Tips

You have **3 minutes**. Here's a template:

1. **What is it?** (15 seconds) — One sentence about what your project does
2. **Live demo** (90 seconds) — Show it working. If it's slow on Pi, that's fine — the audience gets it
3. **How it works** (45 seconds) — Quick architecture: what model, what hardware, any clever tricks
4. **What surprised you** (30 seconds) — What was harder/easier than expected?

Don't spend time on slides. Live demos are more interesting.

---

## Awards

Voted on by the audience at the end of the showcase:

| Award | What It Recognizes |
|---|---|
| **Best Overall** | Best combination of usefulness, polish, and creativity |
| **Most Creative** | Most unexpected or imaginative use of the tech |
| **Best Beginner Project** | Best project from someone new to AI/edge computing |
| **"How Did You Even Do That?"** | Most technically impressive or surprising achievement |
| **Best Use of Multimodal** | Best use of Gemma 4's vision, audio, or video capabilities |
